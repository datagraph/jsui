Hello, There

This is a book about a thing called Dydra Studio. It lives in your web browser, which is rather nice because that means you don't have to go looking for it in a drawer somewhere.

Dydra Studio helps you keep track of data. Data is a word for things you know and want to remember. The data lives in little collections called repositories, which is a rather grand word for "a place where things are kept." (Like a cupboard, only for information instead of jam.)

What Can It Do?

Dydra Studio is what clever people call a "single-page web application," which means that once you've arrived, you don't have to keep going somewhere else. Everything happens right where you are, which is much more comfortable.

Here are the sorts of things you can do:

  • Keep your data in repositories (safe and sound)
  • Ask questions about your data (and get answers, usually)
  • Bring data in from other places (imports)
  • Take data out to share with friends (exports)
  • Save your favourite questions so you can ask them again (views)
  • Change the things written about yourself
  • Share with others (if you'd like to)

Before We Begin

Finding the Door

To get to Dydra Studio, you'll need to visit this address:

The Address: https://dydra.com/ui/user

Type that into your browser, and there you'll be. It's rather like knowing the right path to someone's house.

Your Browser

Two Kinds of Doors

There are actually two ways into the system:

  • The User App: This is the friendly door for everyday people. That's the one we'll talk about.
  • The Console: This is for administrators, who have to do important-sounding things. There's another book about that.

A Quick Start (For Those in a Hurry)

If you're the sort who likes to get straight to things:

  1. Go to https://dydra.com/ui/user
  2. Tell it who you are (that's in Getting In)
  3. Do whatever you came to do

There. That wasn't so hard, was it?
