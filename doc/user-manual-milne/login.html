In Which We Knock on the Door

Before you can do anything at all, you must first prove that you are who you say you are. This is called "logging in," and it's rather like showing your invitation at a party. The computer can't just let anyone in, you see. It has to be careful.

Where to Go

The place you need to visit is here:

The Address: https://dydra.com/ui/user

If you haven't logged in yet, you'll see a friendly little form asking you to introduce yourself. If you have logged in (perhaps you forgot?), you'll already be inside, which is rather nice.

How to Get In

Here's what to do, step by step:

Step 1: Go to https://dydra.com/ui/user. Type it into your browser's address bar and press Enter. Off you go!

Figure 1: The login form, all filled in and ready.

Step 2: Fill in the blanks. There are three things to tell it:
  • Host: Usually https://dydra.com (that's probably already there)
  • Login: Your username (the name you chose when you joined)
  • Password: Your secret password (shh, don't tell anyone)
Step 3: Click the Login button. (Or just press Enter‚Äîeither way works perfectly well.)
Step 4: If everything is right, in you go! You'll see your account page, with all your repositories waiting for you. If something was wrong‚Äîperhaps you misremembered your password‚Äîit will tell you, and you can try again. Computers are patient like that.
A Curious Thing: You can actually be logged into more than one account at the same time. Perhaps you have a work account and a home account. The computer doesn't mind at all‚Äîit can keep track of both.

Two Ways to Say Hello

There are two different ways to prove you're you:

  • Username and Password: The usual way. You type your name and your secret word. Humans do this.
  • A Special Token: A long, complicated string of letters and numbers that computers use when talking to each other. You probably won't need to worry about this unless you're writing programs.

Most people just use their username and password, and that works splendidly.

What the Computer Remembers

The Nice Part

The computer remembers your username. The next time you come back, it will already be filled in for you. Isn't that thoughtful?

The Careful Part: The computer does NOT remember your password or your login session. When you close the browser tab, poof! You're logged out. This is for your own protection, really. If someone else came along after you left, they couldn't just pretend to be you.

Saying Goodbye

When you're done and want to leave properly:

  1. Find the Logout button (it's usually up at the top somewhere)
  2. Click it
  3. You'll be back at the login page, safe and sound
  4. Your username might still be there, waiting for next time

If the Door Won't Open

When You Can't Get In

Sometimes things don't work. Here are some things to check:

  • Did you type your password correctly? (Careful with the Caps Lock key‚Äîit makes everything SHOUT.)
  • Is the Host address right? It should say https://dydra.com
  • Is your internet working? (Try going to another website to check.)
  • Are you sure you have an account?

What the Error Messages Mean

  • "Invalid credentials" means either your username or password was wrong. Try again, more carefully.
  • If nothing seems to work at all, perhaps the internet is having a difficult day.

If you've tried everything and it still won't work, write to support@dydra.com. They'll sort it out.

Now You're In!

Once you've logged in successfully, a whole world of possibilities opens up:

  • Look at your account information (that's you!)
  • Play with your repositories (that's where your data lives)
  • Create and run views (questions for your data)
  • Read some helpful hints

Welcome in. Do make yourself comfortable.
