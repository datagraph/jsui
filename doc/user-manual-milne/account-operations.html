In Which We Learn About Ourselves

Your account is a collection of things that say who you are. It's rather like a little card with your name on it, except it has more information than that, and it lives inside the computer instead of in your pocket.

What You'll Find There

When you look at your account, you'll see all sorts of things about yourself:

  • Your username (the name you use to get in)
  • Your email address (for important messages)
  • Your full name (the one your parents gave you)
  • Your homepage and blog (if you have such things)
  • Your company (where you work)
  • Where you live
  • Your repositories (those come later in the book)

How to Change Them

It's really quite simple:

Step 1: Go to your account page. You can get there by clicking on your name somewhere at the top.
Step 2: Find the thing you want to change.
Step 3: Double-click on it. (That means clicking twice, rather quickly.) It will turn into a little box where you can type.

Figure 1: Double-click, and the box appears. Type something new.

Step 4: Type the new value. Take your time‚Äîspelling counts.
Step 5: Press Enter, or click somewhere else. The typing box goes away.
Step 6: Click the Save button. This is terribly important! If you don't click Save, the computer won't remember what you changed.

Figure 2: The Save button. Do click it.

A Clever Thing: The computer only sends the things you changed, not everything. It's efficient like that‚Äîno sense carrying things that don't need carrying.
Remember! If you forget to click Save, your changes will disappear when you leave the page. They'll be gone, like footprints in the rain. So always, always click Save.

Your Secret Token

You have something called a "token." It's like a special password, but for computer programs to use instead of people. If you write programs that need to talk to Dydra, they'll use this token.

Finding Your Token

  1. Go to your account page
  2. Look for the Token section
  3. There it is!

Getting a New Token

Sometimes you might need a new one. Perhaps you accidentally told someone your old one (oh dear), or perhaps you just want a fresh start.

  1. Find the token section
  2. Click Reset Token
  3. A brand new token appears!
  4. If any programs were using the old one, you'll need to tell them about the new one

Figure 3: The Reset button. Use it wisely.

A Word of Caution: Your token is secret, just like a password. Don't tell it to anyone you don't trust completely. If someone else has your token, they can pretend to be you. If you think someone might have seen it, reset it right away.

As we mentioned before, you can be logged into several accounts at once. Perhaps you have different accounts for different purposes‚Äîone for work, one for play. The computer keeps track of them all, each in its own little space.

  • Switch between accounts whenever you like
  • Work with different repositories in each
  • Each account has its own separate session

When Things Go Wrong

Changes Won't Save

  • Did you click the Save button? (Check again.)
  • Look for any error messages‚Äîthey're trying to help
  • Make sure you're allowed to change this account
  • Is your internet still working?

Can't Find Your Token

  • Are you looking at the right account?
  • Try refreshing the page
  • Check that you have the right permissions
