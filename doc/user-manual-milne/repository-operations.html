In Which We Find Places to Keep Things

A repository is a place where data lives. Think of it as a cozy little house for your information‚Äîa place where facts and figures can settle down and be comfortable. You can have as many repositories as you like, each with its own name and its own collection of things inside.

Making a New Repository

If you'd like a new place to keep some data, here's how to make one:

Step 1: Go to your account page. That's where all your repositories are listed.
Step 2: Find the New Repository button and give it a click.
Step 3: A little window will appear, asking you for a name. Think of a good name‚Äîsomething that will remind you what's inside. You can also choose a type:
  • lmdb: An ordinary repository (this is the usual choice)
  • lmdb-revisioned: A special repository that remembers what things used to be, like a diary

Figure 1: Give your new repository a name.

Step 4: Click OK (or press Enter).
Step 5: And there it is! Your new repository, all ready for data.
About Names: Repository names can have letters, numbers, hyphens (-), and underscores (_). No spaces, though. Spaces confuse computers. Keep it simple.

What's Inside a Repository

When you look at a repository, you'll see:

  • The name (you chose this)
  • A summary (a few words about what's inside)
  • A description (more words, if needed)
  • A homepage (a web address, if there is one)
  • How many quads are in there (quads are the little bits of data)
  • How much space it takes up
  • License information
  • Whether anything is being imported right now
  • Views (questions you've saved)

Putting Things In (Importing)

Data can come from files on your computer or from addresses on the internet.

Types of Files

  • Turtle (.ttl) ‚Äî A friendly format
  • RDF/XML (.rdf, .xml) ‚Äî An older format
  • N-Triples (.nt) ‚Äî Very simple, one thing per line
  • N-Quads (.nq) ‚Äî Like N-Triples, but with an extra bit
  • TriG (.trig) ‚Äî Like Turtle, but fancier
  • JSON-LD (.jsonld, .json) ‚Äî A modern format
  • CSV (.csv) ‚Äî Like a spreadsheet

From Your Computer

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Click Import.
Step 3: Choose a file from your computer.

Figure 4: Choose your file.

Step 4: The computer figures out what kind of file it is.
Step 5: Click Import.
Step 6: Wait patiently. It will tell you when it's done.

From the Internet

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Click Import.
Step 3: Type in the web address where the data lives.

Figure 5: Type the web address.

Step 4: You might need to tell it what kind of file it is.
Step 5: Click Import.
Patience: Big imports can take a while. The computer can work on them in the background while you do other things. It doesn't mind.

Sharing with Friends (Collaborators)

You can let other people look at your repository, or even change things in it.

Adding a Friend

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find the Collaboration section.
Step 3: Click Add Collaborator.
Step 4: Type their username.

Figure 7: Add a friend to your repository.

Step 5: Choose what they can do:
  • Read: They can look, but not change things
  • Write: They can look AND change things
Step 6: Click Add.
Step 7: Click Save. (Always Save!)

Removing a Friend

If you need to remove someone's access, find them in the list, click the delete button (it looks like a little rubbish bin), confirm, and Save.

When Things Go Wrong

Import Won't Work

  • Is it a supported file type?
  • Is the file valid?
  • Is your internet working?
  • Do you have permission to write to this repository?

Export Won't Work

  • Is there actually data in there?
  • Try a different format
  • Check your browser's download settings
