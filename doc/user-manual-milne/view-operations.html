In Which We Learn to Ask Questions

A view is a question you've saved so you can ask it again later. Because sometimes you think of a very good question, and it would be a shame to forget it. The question is written in a special language called SPARQL (which is pronounced "sparkle," like something that glitters).

What Views Are For

Views are rather useful:

  • You can save questions you ask often
  • You can share them with friends
  • You can ask them in different ways and get different sorts of answers
  • You can keep them all together in one repository, nice and tidy

Making a New View

Here's how to save a new question:

Step 1: Go to the repository where you want to keep this question.
Step 2: Find the views section.
Step 3: Click New View.
Step 4: Give your question a name. Something that will remind you what it's about.

Figure 1: Name your question.

Step 5: Press Enter.
Step 6: A little message pops up to say it worked.

Figure 2: It worked! Now write the actual question.

Step 7: An editor appears. That's where you write your question.
Important: Your new view is empty right now. It's like a blank piece of paper. You need to write something on it and save it, or it won't do anything at all.

Writing Your Question (In the Repository)

You can write your question right there in the repository pane.

Opening the Editor

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Click on the view's name.
Step 3: An editor opens up. It's a nice place to write SPARQL.

Writing the Question

Step 1: Find the big text area where you can type.
Step 2: Write your SPARQL question. The editor will colour different parts different colours, which is helpful for seeing what's what.

Figure 3: Write your question here.

Step 3: Click Save to remember it forever (or at least until you change it).
Step 4: You'll see a message saying it saved. Hooray!
A Shortcut: If you press Ctrl+Enter (or Cmd+Enter on a Mac), the question runs immediately. You don't have to save it first‚Äîthough saving is still a good idea.

Writing in Its Own Window

If you'd like more room to write, you can give the view its very own pane.

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find your view.
Step 3: Click the little pane button (it looks like a folder with a plus).
Step 4: A whole new pane opens, just for this view. So much room!

Figure 4: A room of one's own.

Step 5: Write your question.
Step 6: Click Save.
Multiple Windows: You can have lots of editors open at once, some in the repository pane and some in their own panes. They don't interfere with each other at all.

Asking the Question (Running a View)

Once you've written a question, you can ask it and get an answer.

From the Editor

Step 1: Open the view in an editor.
Step 2: Make sure your question looks right.
Step 3: Choose what shape you'd like the answer in (there's a little dropdown menu).

Figure 5: Ready to ask!

Step 4: Click Run (or press Ctrl+Enter).
Step 5: The answer appears below. There it is!

Seeing Answers in a New Window

Sometimes it's nice to see results in their very own window, separate from everything else.

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find your view.
Step 3: Click the window button (it looks like a little link).

Figure 6a: The window button.

Step 4: A brand new window opens with your results in a table!

Figure 6b: Results in their own window.

Step 5: You can choose different answer shapes from a menu in the new window.
Popups: If nothing happens when you click, your browser might be blocking new windows. You'll need to tell it that popups from this site are all right.

Deleting a View

Sometimes a question isn't useful anymore, and you'd like to tidy it away.

Be Sure: When you delete a view, it's gone forever. If you might want the question again someday, copy it somewhere safe first.
Step 1: Find the view.
Step 2: Click the delete button (the little rubbish bin).
Step 3: A message appears, asking if you're sure.

Figure 7: Are you quite sure?

Step 4: If you're sure, click to confirm.
Step 5: The view is gone.

Nice Things the Editor Can Do

Multiple Tabs

The editor can have several tabs, like a notebook with different pages:

  • Each tab has its own question
  • Each tab keeps its own answers
  • You can flip between them to compare
  • Close tabs you don't need anymore

Reset

If you've made a mess of your question, click Reset to go back to how it was before you started changing things.

The Log

There's a little log that remembers what happened‚Äîquestions you asked, things you saved, any mistakes that occurred, and how long things took.

Dragging to a New Window

You can grab an editor tab and drag it right out of the browser window, and it becomes its own little window. Rather magical, really.

Some Advice About Views

  • Names: Give views sensible names that remind you what they're about
  • Organization: Keep related views in the same repository
  • Comments: You can write notes in your SPARQL using # marks
  • Testing: Try your question before you save it
  • Backups: Keep copies of important questions somewhere safe

When Things Go Wrong

Won't Save

  • Did you click Save?
  • Check the log for error messages
  • Do you have permission?
  • Is your internet working?

Won't Run

  • Is there a mistake in your SPARQL?
  • Are the addresses in your question right?
  • Look at the log for clues
  • Is there actually data to ask about?

View Missing

  • Try refreshing the page
  • Did it save properly?
  • Are you looking in the right repository?
