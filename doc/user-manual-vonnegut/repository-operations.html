The Boxes

Repositories are boxes. You put data in them. Sometimes you take data out. That's pretty much the whole story, but people have written entire books about less interesting things.

Making a New Box

So you want a new repository. Good for you.

Step 1: Go to your account page. Click your name somewhere up there.
Step 2: Find the New Repository button. Click it.
Step 3: A little window appears. Type a name. Pick a type if you feel like it:
  • lmdb: Regular box
  • lmdb-revisioned: Box that remembers what used to be in it

Figure 1: The little window. Type something.

Step 4: Press Enter or click OK.
Step 5: Congratulations. You have a box now.
Names: Letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores. No spaces. No poems. Keep it simple.

What's in the Box?

When you look at a repository, you'll see things:

  • The name (you picked this)
  • A summary (you might have written this)
  • A description (maybe you wrote this too)
  • The homepage (if there is one)
  • How many quads are in there
  • How much disk space it's eating
  • License stuff
  • Whether anything is being imported right now
  • Views (queries you saved)

Putting Things In

Data goes in. That's the import part.

What Kinds of Data

  • Turtle (.ttl)
  • RDF/XML (.rdf, .xml)
  • N-Triples (.nt)
  • N-Quads (.nq)
  • TriG (.trig)
  • JSON-LD (.jsonld, .json)
  • CSV (.csv)

Step 1: Go to the repository.

Sharing the Box

Collaborators are people you let look in your box. Sometimes you let them change things too. That's up to you.

Adding Someone

Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find the Collaboration section.
Step 3: Click Add Collaborator.
Step 4: Type their name.

Figure 6: Type their name. Pick their powers.

Step 5: Pick what they can do:
  • Read: Look but don't touch
  • Write: Touch all you want
Step 6: Click Add.
Step 7: Click Save. Always click Save.

About Trust

Some advice:

  • Give people the least power they need
  • Check your list once in a while
  • Read-only is usually enough
  • Write down why each person has access

Trust is a funny thing. You have to give it, but you don't have to give it all at once.

Emptying the Box

Sometimes you want to start over. Clear gets rid of the data but keeps the box.

Warning: This is forever. The data will be gone. Not hiding. Gone.
Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find the Clear button.
Step 3: Click it.

Figure 9: The warning. It means it.

Step 4: Read the warning.
Step 5: Think about it.
Step 6: If you're sure, confirm.

Throwing Away the Box

Delete gets rid of everything. The box, the data, all of it. Gone.

Warning: This is very forever. Everything disappears. The box. The data. The views. The memories. All of it.
Step 1: Go to the repository.
Step 2: Find Delete Repository.
Step 3: Click it.
Step 4: Read the warning. Maybe type the name to prove you mean it.
Step 5: If you're really sure, confirm.
Step 6: The box is gone. You're back at your account page, looking at all your other boxes, wondering if you did the right thing.

When Things Go Wrong

Import Won't Work

  • Is the file format supported?
  • Is the file actually valid?
  • Is your internet working?
  • Do you have write permission?
  • What does the error message say?

Export Won't Work

  • Is there data in there?
  • Try a different format
  • Check your browser downloads
  • Do you have read permission?
