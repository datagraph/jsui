The Door

You can't get in without proving who you are. That's just how it works. The computer doesn't trust you. Why should it? It doesn't know you.

Finding the Door

Go here:

URL: https://dydra.com/ui/user

If you're not logged in, you'll see a login page. If you are logged in, you won't. That's how you know.

Getting In

Here's the ritual:

Step 1: Go to https://dydra.com/ui/user. You just did this. Good.

Figure 1: The login form. Fill in the blanks.

Step 2: Fill in the blanks:
  • Host: Usually https://dydra.com
  • Login: Your username
  • Password: Your password
Step 3: Click Login. Or press Enter. Either works.
Step 4: If you got everything right, you're in. If not, you're not. Try again.
Fun fact: You can be logged into multiple accounts at the same time. The computer can handle it. Computers are good at keeping track of things. Better than people, usually.

Two Ways to Prove You're You

There are two methods:

  • Basic Authentication: Username and password. The classic. Works every time. Well, most times.
  • Bearer Token: A special code for computers talking to computers. Format: "Bearer yourTokenHere"

If you're a human using a browser, stick with the username and password. That's what it's there for.

Memory

What the Computer Remembers

The computer remembers your username. That's it. Just the username.

  • Your username gets saved in the browser
  • Next time you come back, it's already filled in
What it doesn't remember: Your password. Your token. Your session. When you close the browser tab, all of that disappears. Poof. Gone. You'll have to log in again. That's a security feature, not a bug.

Logging Out

When you're done:

  1. Click Logout in the navigation bar
  2. You'll be taken back to the login page
  3. Everything you were doing is gone now
  4. Your username might still be there. Just the username.

When Things Go Wrong

Can't Get In

Check these things:

  • Did you type your password right? Caps lock is a thing.
  • Is the host URL correct? It should be https://dydra.com
  • Is your internet working?
  • Does your account exist?

Error Messages

If you see an error:

  • Read it. It's trying to tell you something.
  • "Invalid credentials" means wrong username or password
  • If nothing works, email support@dydra.com

Now What?

  • Create and manage repositories
